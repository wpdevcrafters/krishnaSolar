How to Finance a Solar System in India: A Complete Guide

Installing a rooftop solar system is one of the best financial decisions an Indian household can make in 2026, but the upfront cost of ₹80,000 to ₹3,00,000 can be a barrier. Solar financing through a dedicated solar loan bridges this gap, letting you pay in manageable EMIs while saving on electricity from day one.

Understanding Solar Loan EMI

A solar loan EMI is calculated using the standard reducing-balance formula:

EMI = P x r x (1 + r)n / ((1 + r)n - 1)
Where: P = Loan Amount, r = Monthly Interest Rate (Annual Rate / 12), n = Total Months

Because the outstanding balance reduces each month as you repay principal, the interest component of each EMI shrinks over time. This is the reducing-balance method used by Indian banks.

PM Surya Ghar Subsidy and Your Solar Loan

The PM Surya Ghar Muft Bijli Yojana offers central government subsidies up to ₹78,000 for residential rooftop solar installations up to 3 kW. This subsidy:

  • Directly reduces the effective cost of your system
  • Can be deducted from the loan principal, reducing your EMI
  • Is credited to your bank account after installation, while some banks offer bridge financing

Comparing Solar Loan Options

The choice of lender significantly impacts your total interest outgo. For a ₹1,00,000 loan over 7 years:

  • At 7% (SBI): EMI around ₹1,511, Total Interest around ₹26,892
  • At 9% (BOB/PNB): EMI around ₹1,603, Total Interest around ₹34,652
  • At 11% (HDFC): EMI around ₹1,699, Total Interest around ₹42,716

The difference between the cheapest and most expensive loan for the same amount is over ₹15,000 in extra interest. Government bank solar loan schemes are usually the best first option.

When Does Solar Become Cash-Flow Positive?

In many Indian states with tariffs above ₹6/unit, electricity savings from a correctly sized system can exceed the monthly EMI. This means the system may become cash-flow positive from the first month.

Our calculator shows this EMI vs savings comparison automatically. A 3 kW system saves approximately ₹2,520/month (3 kW x 4 units/day x 30 days x ₹7/unit). If your EMI is ₹2,000, your net monthly gain is ₹520.
