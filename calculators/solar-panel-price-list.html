Factors That Affect Solar Panel Price in India

📍 Location & State

Prices vary by 5–15% across states due to logistics, local taxes, and DISCOM requirements. Rajasthan, Gujarat, and Maharashtra typically have competitive rates due to high solar activity.

🏭 Brand & Quality

Tier-1 brands like Adani, Tata, and Waaree command a 10–20% premium over local manufacturers. However, they offer better warranties, bankability, and long-term reliability.

⚡ Panel Technology

Bifacial and Half-cut PERC panels generate more units per panel but cost 30–50% more than standard poly panels. They offer better ROI for roofs with space constraints.

🔧 Installation Type

On-grid systems cost 20–30% less than hybrid (battery) systems. Off-grid systems with batteries are the most expensive. On-grid systems are recommended for urban areas with stable grid connectivity.

📦 System Size

Larger systems benefit from economies of scale. The cost per watt decreases as system size increases — a 10kW system typically costs ₹53/W all-inclusive vs ₹65/W for a 1kW system.

🌐 Import Duties

India imposes 40% Basic Customs Duty on imported solar cells and 25% on modules. This has pushed prices up but boosted domestic manufacturing from brands like Adani, Waaree, and Vikram.

Frequently Asked Questions — Solar Panel Price India 2026

In 2026, solar panel prices in India are typically ₹18-₹24/Wp for PERC, ₹20-₹28/Wp for TOPCon, and ₹32-₹45/Wp for premium HJT panels. Complete installed systems usually cost ₹55,000-₹75,000 per kW before subsidy.

A 3kW solar system costs approximately ₹1.80-₹2.10 lakh before subsidy. With the ₹78,000 PM Surya Ghar subsidy, the net cost usually comes to around ₹1.02-₹1.32 lakh.

Popular brands include Adani Solar, Tata Power Solar, Waaree Energies, Vikram Solar, and Luminous. The best option depends on model availability, warranty, installer support, and current regional pricing.

The subsidy is ₹30,000 for 1kW systems, ₹60,000 for 2kW systems, and ₹78,000 for 3kW and above, subject to scheme rules and installation through approved channels.

A 5kW system typically needs 12-15 panels depending on panel wattage. With 400W panels, you need about 12-13 panels and roughly 25-30 square meters of shade-free rooftop area.
