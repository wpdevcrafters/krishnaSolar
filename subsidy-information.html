What Is PM Surya Ghar: Muft Bijli Yojana?

PM Surya Ghar: Muft Bijli Yojana is India's national residential rooftop solar programme. It helps households install grid-connected rooftop solar systems, reduce monthly electricity bills, and access Central Financial Assistance through the official portal.

The scheme is built around residential consumers applying online, receiving DISCOM feasibility approval, selecting a registered vendor, installing eligible equipment, completing net-metering/commissioning, and then receiving the applicable subsidy after verification.

Current Residential Subsidy Slabs

The central subsidy is capacity-based and is most beneficial for systems up to 3 kW, which is the common residential rooftop size range.

System Capacity Subsidy Structure Maximum CFA Typical Use Case
1 kW ₹30,000 per kW ₹30,000 Low-consumption homes
2 kW ₹30,000 per kW ₹60,000 Small families, lower bills
3 kW ₹60,000 for first 2 kW + ₹18,000 for third kW ₹78,000 Most common residential choice
Above 3 kW Subsidy capped at 3 kW ₹78,000 Large homes with higher usage

Note: Final eligibility depends on portal approval, DISCOM rules, consumer category, system commissioning, and use of eligible equipment.

Group Housing Society And RWA Support

Resident Welfare Associations and Group Housing Societies can receive support for rooftop solar used for common facilities such as lifts, water pumps, lighting, security systems, and shared loads.

Common Facility Solar

Support is calculated per kW for common loads rather than individual flat consumption.

Capacity Ceiling

The scheme allows support up to a defined capacity ceiling for eligible group housing/common-area projects.

Lower Maintenance Bills

Societies can reduce recurring electricity costs for shared services and pass savings into maintenance budgets.

Eligibility Checklist Before You Apply

Residential electricity connection in the applicant's name or valid household documentation.
Shadow-free usable rooftop area suitable for the proposed solar capacity.
Application submitted through the official PM Surya Ghar portal.
Technical feasibility approval from the local DISCOM before installation.
Installation through a registered/empanelled vendor listed on the portal.
Eligible DCR/ALMM-compliant solar modules where required under programme rules.

Application Process

1

Register Online

Use your mobile number, state, DISCOM, and electricity consumer details on the official PM Surya Ghar portal.

2

Apply For Rooftop Solar

Enter roof details, preferred capacity, and electricity connection information for feasibility review.

3

Get DISCOM Feasibility

Wait for technical approval before placing the final installation order.

4

Select Registered Vendor

Choose a portal-listed vendor and confirm equipment, structure, warranty, and net-metering scope.

5

Install And Commission

Complete installation, inspection, net-metering, and commissioning documentation.

6

Claim Subsidy

Submit bank details and required proof on the portal so the approved CFA can be released after verification.

Documents Commonly Needed

  • Latest electricity bill with consumer number and DISCOM details.
  • Aadhaar-linked mobile number and applicant identity details.
  • Bank account details for subsidy disbursement.
  • Rooftop ownership/permission details where applicable.
  • Vendor invoice, installation photos, commissioning certificate, and net-metering details after installation.

DCR, ALMM And Vendor Requirements

For subsidy-linked residential rooftop solar, homeowners should verify that the vendor, inverter, module selection, and documentation match current portal and DISCOM requirements before installation starts.

Using non-compliant equipment or installing outside the approved vendor/process flow can delay approval or make the project ineligible for subsidy. Always confirm the portal status of the vendor and equipment before making payments.
